Beda's Essay

When I enrolled in this course, I was initially most excited about being able to expand my knowledge and skills in coding and programming. Before taking this course, I had little experience with HTML and CSS and was completely unfamiliar with PHP. However, as I navigated through the weekly lectures, I became more intrigued with learning more about bias in technology, and how it interferes with and disables certain groups of people from being able to access and properly execute the functionality of technology. I was familiar with how people from minority backgrounds had trouble accessing the internet or technology, but I wasn’t aware until this course that even when these groups were able to get online, there would be even more bias and obstacles for them to overcome. As the course progressed and was coming to an end, we were told that we would need to design a website and survey that addresses the issues of bias that we have learned throughout the course, and I was excited to learn how my peers perceived bias in the tech industry, especially in regards to gender.

At the start of this project, I constantly referenced back to the readings that were assigned from Algorithms of Oppression. In the first chapter, Saafiyah Noble focused on how search engines continue to uphold stereotypes of women and their “inability” to be anything more than a housewife or submissive to the men in their lives (p. 15). In the second chapter, Noble decided to focus on women of color who were constantly being sexualized on the internet, with a simple search of “black girls” resulting in pages holding titles such as “Black Booty on the Beach” and other illicit content (p.64). It seemed as though the internet was out to hurt women, and when women tried to enter the industry and make a change, they faced even more oppression from those in their workplace. That’s why my group decided to focus our survey on gender bias in the tech industry, as we wanted to know how one can have a bias against women as they try to make an advancement in the tech industry but continuously face obstacles in being able to do so. We wanted to ask questions as to how one perceives a woman’s contribution and role in the tech industry, as well as the hardships she may have on the journey there and once she finally gets into the tech industry. Most of our test takers turned out to be men, and it helped us to gain an understanding of how the opposite sex views women in the tech world and how much of a bias they subconsciously hold.

Working in a remote-paired programming session made me more aware of these faults in the tech world by allowing me to see it first-hand and behind the scenes. After learning the basics of HTML, CSS, and PHP, I could understand how it can be difficult to ensure that everything functions correctly and is able to be accessed by all groups of people. However, it also made me realize that there are easy solutions to these problems, and when it comes to design, you have to take into consideration your audience at the very beginning to make sure that you’re designing inclusively. That’s why for our project, we tried to make our website layout as accessible as possible, making sure that it was easy to navigate and understand. We took into account the feedback we received from our peers as they were our initial audience, and it helped our group to make improvements so that we could have an inclusive design and inclusive questions in our survey. However, there were some difficulties working remotely, as it was hard to ensure that every member of our group was on the same page in our understanding of how we wanted our website to come across and what our goal was. It was even more difficult when we struggled to find a way to host our website while also letting us all have access to the coding files so we could all contribute to the layout of the website equally. In the end, however, we were able to come together as a group and work well together in explaining to one another how we envisioned our site. Additionally, we always addressed any concerns we had and were able to fix them head-on, with each of us always trying to find a solution to our problems.

Overall, this course has allowed me to gain an understanding of how the tech industry works and the bias that is being held in such a world. I hope that the knowledge and skills that I’ve learned in regard to coding can help me to ensure that whatever designs I make in the future take into account all groups of people, making it inclusive and easily accessible as much as possible.
